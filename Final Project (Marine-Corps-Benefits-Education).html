EDUCATION BENEFITS


A CULTURE OF CONTINUAL LEARNING

                    The Marine Corps thinks that a greater education results in a Marine who is even more capable. 

                    We convert our ideas into tools, allowing us to operate above everyone, and make solutions for every challenge. 
                    
                    Marines have access to educational possibilities that will better the combat force that they were yesterday.

BENEFITS IN SUPPORT OF OUR NATION

                    Marines are dedicated to getting the knowledge they need to further themselves and our country.

                    As we triumph in conflicts both within and beyond the classroom, we hold ourselves to the greatest standards.

                    By funding up to 100% of your tuition costs through federal government programs like Tuition Assistance, 
                    the Montgomery GI Bill, and the Marine Corps College Fund, if you join us, we will invest in your education.

A CULTURE OF CONTINUAL LEARNING

                    Winning complex battles on the modern battlefield requires a highly educated force, 
                    and it is for this reason that the Corps invests in the education of those who commit to fighting for our Nation. 
                    
                    The Marine Corps will give you every opportunity to sharpen your mind while you advance your education. 
                    
                    As a Marine, you will be encouraged to pursue your college degree while on active duty,
                    or even after you complete your service and return to civilian life, so that you're just as adept at leading in communities as you are on battlefields. 
                     
                    In fact, many Marines start obtaining college credits immediately, even while in recruit training. 
                     
                    Continuing your education is a vital part of being fighting fit, and the Marine Corps will make every opportunity available
                    to help you achieve your educational goals as a United States Marine.

EDUCATIONAL OPPORTUNITIES

                    As Marines, we take the necessary steps to ensure we know more today than we did yesterday, so that our Nation can also continuously advance.
                    
                    The Marine Corps ensures all Marines have the means to achieve a higher education through the following programs:

                    TUITION ASSISTANCE PROGRAM
                        Up to $225 per college credit and/or $4500 annually, 100% of your tuition at qualified universities is covered while you're in the military. 
                        Any duty station can participate in the Tuition Assistance Program, and doing so could potentially increase your chances of getting promoted. 
                        Marines must maintain a "C" grade point average while enrolled in order to continue earning these perks.

                    ADVANCED DEGREE PROGRAM (ADP)
                        Marines who already hold an undergraduate degree can get some help paying for their master's degree through the ADP. 
                        Marines who are enrolled in graduate school full-time will continue to receive their normal salary and benefits.

                    DEFENSE ACTIVITY FOR NONTRADITIONAL EDUCATION SUPPORT (DANTES)
                    Through DANTES programs, Marines can pursue college degrees via correspondence learning.
                     Earn college credits through exams, outside degree programs, and independent study, among other specifics of the program.

                    Receive unquestionable credit for finishing correspondence courses
                    
                    choose courses from 41 prestigious colleges
                    
                    • DANTES and the Tuition Assistance Program (TAP) are compatible

POST 9/11 GI BILL

                    After 36 months of active duty, Marines who want to use the POST 9/11 GI Bill to pay for their school will receive 100% tuition support. 

                    Marines who meet the basic requirements—90 total days of service—will be eligible for financial aid for tuition, fees, books, and supplies. 
                   
                    While on college, you could be qualified for a monthly housing allowance. Additionally, you might be able to transfer your entitlements to a dependant.

MONTGOMERY GI BILL

                    Marines can obtain tax-free aid toward a college education because to the Montgomery GI Bill. 
                    You may join full- or part-time for about $100 a month for a year. 
                    
                    After serving for 24 months on active service, money becomes available for college or university tuition, vocational school tuition, or both.

THE SPECIAL EDUCATION PROGRAM

                    Through this program, Marines are given the chance to pursue a postgraduate degree in a variety of fields,
                    including management, engineering, communication, and communications. 
                     
                    It permits Marines to pursue advanced degrees at authorized universities in certain Military Occupational
                    Specialties (MOS) while still getting all of the Marine Corps' perks.

STAFF NONCOMMISSIONED OFFICER’S DEGREE COMPLETION PROGRAM

                    Through this program for educational benefits, Marines can finish their degrees while continuing on active
                    service by enrolling full-time at a recognized four-year university. 
                    
                    While still earning full pay and allowances from the Marine Corps, these perks must be earned.

                    Marines who have attained the rank of staff sergeant or above and who have acquired enough college credits
                    to finish a B.S. or B.A. in 18 months on campus are eligible for this program. After graduation, a four-year commitment is necessary.
