    MARINE CORPS PENSION - FUTURE SECURITY

                        Marines are eligible for retirement benefits and a pension after 20 years of active duty service, which allows than most in the civilian world.

                    Knowing they have the financial stability of a pension, many retired Marines maintain active occupations in their communities after serving in the Marines.

    THROUGH THE RANKS

                        Enlisted Marines and Marine Officers each have their own chain of command within the Marine Corps.

                    Enlistees make up the majority of Marines. Private, Private First Class, and Lance Corporal are the lowest enlisted Marine ranks. 

                    Each level has a paygrade that corresponds to it, ranging from private (E-1) through E-9.


                        To become a noncommissioned officer, or NCO, is the next big step after lance corporal.

                        Corporal comes first in the NCO ranks, followed by sergeant and finally staff NCOs, which are the highest levels in the Corps.
                        
                        Staff sergeant, gunnery sergeant, master sergeant, and first sergeant are the staff NCO levels, respectively. 
                        
                        The Sergeant Major of the Marine Corps, who works alongside the Commandant, is the highest ranked enlisted Marine.

                        No of their rank, every Marine enlisted is entrusted with the duty of protecting the wellbeing of our nation both today and in the future.

    POSITIONED TO LEADPOSITIONED TO LEAD

                        A tremendous honor and a great dedication to the Marine Corps, our country, and its citizens are required to be commissioned as a Marine officer. 
                    
                    No matter their rank, Marine Officers are expected to lead Marines into battle and secure victories on both the present and future fronts.


                        The first three officer levels are second lieutenant, first lieutenant, and captain, individuals who attain them are known as company-grade officers.

                        Majors join lieutenant colonels and colonels as field-grade officers upon promotion to the rank, it's a critical turning point for Marine officers. 

                        The rank of colonel is the highest promotion available to field-grade officers. Being promoted to the position of general is an honor. 
                        
                        Brigadier general, major general, lieutenant general, and general are among the general positions that are linked to one-star to four-star rankings.

                        Four-star general, sometimes known as general, is the highest officer rank. These senior officials are proposed by the President. 
                        
                        The Commandant of the Marine Corps, who's highest senior Marine Officer, is a four-star general who sits on the Joint Chiefs of Staff.
